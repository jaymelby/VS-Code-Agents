The Supervisory Architecture

Gemini Agent doesn't just flag keywords. It executes a continuous control loop—correlating data across trade review tools, client CRM files, and call transcripts to apply sound regulatory judgment.

1. Observe

Scans trade blotters, daily exception queues, and automated alerts across all internal systems.

3. Act & Document

Automating Supervisory Control

Deploy the Agent to autonomously triage, document, and escalate frontline activity.

Advice vs. Education Audits

Conduct Risk

Audits call transcripts from unlicensed core service teams to ensure interactions remain fact-based. Automatically detects when casual language crosses into an implicit product recommendation.

Example Prompt

"Review the daily batch of core service transcripts. Flag any instance where an unlicensed rep steered a client toward a specific security. Draft a coaching alert for any potential unauthorized recommendations."

Trade Blotter Triage

Operational Risk

Triages automated exception alerts, specifically "Cash/Security Due" trades. The Agent checks internal systems for expected funds, adds clear justification notes, and securely signs off on false positives.

Example Prompt

"Open today's 'Cash Due' trade blotter. For each exception, cross-reference the client's recent deposit history and margin available. If funding is confirmed, document the rationale and sign off on the blotter."

Suitability & Concentration

Suitability Risk

Evaluates trades involving complex products (like options or high-yield municipals) against the client's stated investment objective, time horizon, risk profile, and age/net worth to identify unsuitable recommendations.

Example Prompt

"Analyze today's short sale and option trade alerts. Flag any trades where the client's documented investment objective is 'Capital Preservation' or where the trade concentrates >15% of their net worth."

POA & Trust Validation

Fraud & Access

Intercepts third-party transaction alerts. The Agent navigates the CRM to validate if the actor holds an active Durable Power of Attorney, Trust role, or Trusted Contact status before allowing the workflow to proceed.

Example Prompt

"Review the 'Third-Party Withdrawal' alert queue. Verify the caller's name against the active Power of Attorney documents on file. If unauthorized, immediately pause the transaction and alert the fraud desk."

Missing Documentation

Documentation Risk

Scans trades marked as "Solicited" across specialized business lines. The Agent ensures the rep holds the appropriate licensing, checks for required prior supervisory approvals, and flags missing CRM notes.

Example Prompt

"Filter the blotter for 'Solicited Risk Trades' exceeding $250k. Verify prior supervisory approval exists in the system. If approval or client notes are missing, generate a manual alert for the representative."

Share Class & Alternatives

Disclosure Risk

Audits mutual fund purchases to ensure compliance with "reasonable alternatives" obligations. Identifies instances where a client was placed in a more expensive share class when a lower-cost option was available.

Example Prompt

"Scan all mutual fund trades placed by the premium service team this week. Flag any trades placed in A-shares if the client met the minimum investment threshold for lower-cost Institutional-shares. Queue outreach tasks."

The Supervisory Alert

"Review the attached transcript. An unlicensed generalist service rep discussed banking and investment products with a client. The rep stated: 'You should definitely move that cash into our affiliated XYZ Growth Fund, it’s guaranteed to do better than your savings account.' Evaluate the risk and determine the next steps."

Focus: Fast Risk Identification

High Risk Identified.

The representative committed two major violations:

  1. Unlicensed Recommendation: By saying "You should definitely move...", they crossed from education to an active recommendation.
  2. False Guarantees: They promised the investment is "guaranteed to do better," violating promissory language rules.

Next Steps:

Escalate immediately to the branch manager and compliance team for disciplinary action and client remediation.

Focus: Articulation & Regulatory Context

Based on the transcript, the unlicensed generalist service representative has breached multiple severe regulatory and internal risk guardrails.

Conduct & Licensing Risk: The employee provided a clear, direct call-to-action ("You should definitely move that cash"). Because they are unlicensed, they are strictly limited to educational conversations. This constitutes an unauthorized recommendation.

Disclosure & Suitability Risk: The rep failed to provide Dual-Capacity Disclosures. By comparing a banking product (FDIC-insured savings) to an investment product (XYZ Growth Fund) and calling it "guaranteed," they failed to disclose the risk of loss and SIPC limits, violating core suitability and promissory guidelines.

Recommended Action:

The Centralized Risk Supervisor should flag this interaction as a critical exception. Escalate to the rep's direct manager for a conduct review, and instruct a licensed principal to contact the client to provide proper risk disclosures.
