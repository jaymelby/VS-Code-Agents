# Centralized Risk Supervisor Role Summary

## Overview
- The Centralized Risk Supervisor is a senior supervisory role responsible for performing assigned oversight activities in accordance with internal policies, procedures, and applicable industry rules and regulations.
- The role includes supervisory review of account exceptions, trading activity, and correspondence, as needed.
- This position requires strong cross-functional partnership skills and the ability to build and maintain effective working relationships with colleagues, business partners, and senior leadership.
- Success in the role depends on sound knowledge of regulatory and supervisory expectations, strong analytical judgment, and the ability to work effectively in a fast-paced environment with multiple competing priorities.

## Primary Responsibilities
- Provide day-to-day supervision of designated activities.
- Identify, escalate, and address issues, emerging trends, and outlier behaviors.
- Provide guidance on relevant policies, procedures, and supervisory expectations.
- Support consistent execution of oversight responsibilities across assigned business areas.

## Core Capabilities
- Ability to analyze data and identify meaningful trends.
- Ability to recognize key risks and determine appropriate resolution steps.
- Strong collaboration skills across multiple levels of management.
- Strong judgment and decision-making skills based on available information and business context.
- Ability to manage multiple priorities under tight deadlines.
- Ability to work effectively in a high-volume, fast-paced supervisory environment.

## Qualifications
- 5+ years of supervision experience.
- Strong communication, influencing, and relationship-management skills.
- Strong understanding of compliance and regulatory requirements relevant to the brokerage and investment advisory industry.
- Strong time management, organization, and prioritization skills.
- Ability to work under intensive deadlines.
- Bachelor’s degree or equivalent work experience.

## Licensing / Credential Expectations
- Active brokerage and supervisory licenses required.
- Additional advisory or product-specific licenses preferred, depending on business scope.
- Specialized licenses related to futures, forex, or other products may be preferred based on role coverage.

## What the Role Is Accountable For
- Ensuring supervisory reviews are completed consistently and in alignment with policy and regulatory requirements.
- Detecting and escalating risks, trends, and unusual activity.
- Serving as a control point between frontline activity, business partners, and leadership.
- Bringing structure, oversight, and risk awareness to supervisory processes.

## Ideal Candidate Profile
- Experienced supervisor with strong regulatory and operational knowledge.
- Comfortable working across teams and functions to resolve issues.
- Skilled at identifying risks, interpreting patterns, and escalating concerns appropriately.
- Able to balance speed, accuracy, and sound judgment in a deadline-driven environment.
- Effective communicator who can influence partners and leaders while maintaining a strong control mindset.
